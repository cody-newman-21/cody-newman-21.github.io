Learning Plan

Part 1: Formulating what it will take for me to be learning to the best of my ability...

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

To get to a point where I can find the ultimate work/lifestyle balance. To be able to work remotely and choose my own hours of work, while working on projects that are meaningful and can make a positive impact in peoples lives.

A description of your own strengths and limitations in relation to learning?

Im good with communication and being patient in learning new things. I also find it easiest to learn when I can be more hands-on and apply the skills to real situations, so these first two weeks have been a great deep dive learning experience.
My limitations are that I don’t learn too well by sitting and listening as my attention can start to wander. I also find I can go down the rabbit-hole on particular things quite easily and end up falling behind.

What skills (non-technical - human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

My confidence in myself. The ability to deal with pressure without getting overwhelmed, stressed or anxious. To be punctual, responsible and dependable.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme

I plan to get into the routine of getting up early and treating the day and workload as if it were a real job. This will mean not staying up too late and getting enough sleep so that I’m ready to wake up and get into it in the morning. If I find myself getting too stressed out, I will remind myself to remain calm, focus on my breathing and go take a break. I've noticed if I take a break and go for some fresh air, a walk, or even just a quick stretch - I can come back to the work feeling much more clear headed and ready to take on the problem more systematically.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

I will speak up sooner if I’m really stuck and reach out to my fellow students and facilitators through the slack channel.

A description of what you expect from the facilitation team

I'd expect them to be supportive and understanding, and from what I've seen so far, they are. I had a really great tutor during my last course (Small business management/project management) who was able to adapt to every individual students needs and make sure nobody got left behind. I’d like to be able to get a quick response to a question, even a quick video call to be able to discuss things easier. But I understand that everyone is busy and so I won't rely on that.

Part 2: End of foundations reflections and update to learning plan...

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

My long term goal is very much still the same. I'd like to be able to have the ultimate work/lifestyle balance and be able to put my skills to use in a meaningful way to help better peoples lives, ideally while being able to work remotely. The skills I hope to gain from this course are basically a solid understanding of the programming languages we will be taught so I can futher improve and focus on them and better understand other languages after the bootcamp.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

My strengths and limitations are very much the same, although I've realised two big things during foundations - that another strength of mine is being able to work in a team, as I recalled working with others in my previous job and how effective that was towards my learning and work output. On the other hand, I realised this through one of my limitations - That working and in particular learning by myself isn't as easy as I first thought. I tend to look into things too much and get stuck on the detail and without having someone to talk to and bounce ideas off or guide me, I get overwhelmed and lost. From what I've heard about the structure of bootcamp I think ultimately these things will help me.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

I think my biggest non-technical challenges will be staying focused and putting my time to good use, as well as making sure I get enough sleep to be learning at my best, by not staying up too late and getting distracted. I plan to really make an effort as it is something I need to work on, not just for the bootcamp but for going forwards in life after the bootcamp too.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

As stated above in my strengths and limitations, I would like to work on my ability to stay focused and to get a good nights sleep each night. I realise how important both of them are and that staying focused is heavily based in getting a good nights sleep. Other than this, I would also like to improve on my ability to communicate while under pressure and speak up when im unsure.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

I would hope that they're understanding of each students individual needs and abilities. I worry that I may not be able to keep up but I dont want to be left behind, although I realise this is mostly up to me and the effort I put in. I would also expect that the team do their best to explain concepts so as not to leave the students with holes of important information left out. In this regard I think context is highly important.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

My expectations are that I will get out what I put in. When I need to, I will try to seek help through my peers, tutors or through google. I understand the next 9 weeks could be very intense at times so I will try not to be too hard on myself and I'll try to make sure im enjoying myself too.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme

I intend to get to sleep before midnight and be up at 8am to make sure I get at least 8 hours of sleep each night. I will eat well and exercise regularly to make sure my body and mind are performing at their best for learning and working with others. If I get stressed out or overwhelmed outside of course hours I will take a break to rest my mind. I would like to start reading before bed as I understand the benefits of this, but will explore this more once I've gotten started with the bootcamp.
